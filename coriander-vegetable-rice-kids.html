Coriander Vegetable Rice for Kids

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup long grain rice
  • 1/2 cup chopped vegetables of your choice. (I have used green peas and carrots)
  • 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1/2 inch ginger piece
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/4 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2-3 black pepper corns
  • 1-2 clove
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon ghee
  • Salt to taste

Method:

1. Wash the rice thoroughly, drain and keep aside for 10 minutes. Roughly chop the ginger and garlic .

2. In a grinder jar , pulse the coriander leaves, ginger and garlic to make a paste. Add 1-2 teaspoon water if needed.

3. Heat a pressure cooker on a medium flame and melt ghee. Add the whole spices and saute for 30 seconds.

4. Add onion and stir fry until it turns soft. Add vegetables and saute for another 1-2 minutes.

5. Now add the the prepared green paste and saute well.

6. At this stage add the turmeric powder and salt followed by rice. Stir fry everything well for 1-2 minutes.

7. Lastly add 2 cups water , stir well. You can do a taste test at this point. Adjust the seasonings if desired.

8. Cover the pressure cooker with a lid and cook for 2 whistles. Turn off the flame. Allow the pressure to release naturally. Open the lid, fluff the rice with a fork .

9. Serve warm with salad and curd/ raita.

The coriander vegetable rice should be given to kids while warm and consumed the same day. While it is a great option for the lunchbox, it can also be served at lunch or dinner with some salad and curd. The whole spices add a beautiful flavor to the rice, and this recipe can be served to kids above 2 years of age. And since itâ€™s a one pot recipe, it makes life easier for Moms!
