Top 10 Home Remedies for Diarrhea in Children

October 13, 2014 21 Comments

Stopping children from playing is impossible, and getting infected due to exposure to outside environment is inevitable. Cold, cough, fever, diarrhea and worms are some of the common ailments that children suffer from periodically.

I have listed a few food modifications in this article to be used during diarrhea. Nourishing your child correctly and using natural kitchen remedies for common ailments reduces the need for heavy medications and antibiotics. Moreover, using natural food as medicines boosts your child’s natural immunity and helps ward off infections effectively.

What Causes Diarrhea?

The major cause of Diarrhea in children is due to

  • Infected food and water.
  • Putting dirty fingers in the mouth.
  • Eating roadside food.
  • Eating food prepared in unfiltered water.
  • Using unclean vegetables/ fruits/meat in the diet cause intestinal distress and prevent the absorption of nutrients by the gut.

Diarrhoea is the body’s defense mechanism to throw out infections/ microbes from the gut. However, the body also throws out fluid, electrolytes, and nutrients in the process of eliminating infection; which causes weakness and malnutrition in the bargain.

Home Remedies for Diarrhea

Food given during such episodes must be fluid & electrolyte-rich, must be easy to digest and at the same time must be nourishing to the body.

Diarrhea may be of 2 types –

1. Infective

2. Mucous

Home remedies for Diarrhoea – Infective

1. Stewed apple with cinnamon & honey

The pectin in apple (especially after stewing) helps bind stools and absorbs excessive water from the gut, thus controlling diarrhea.

2. Plenty of seasoned curds

Seasoned curd works well against infective bacteria in the gut. It also aids in relieving any stomachache or cramps during diarrhea.

3. Coconut water

Electrolyte liquids are a must to ensure that the body is well hydrated.

4. Lemon sherbet

They help in restoring the hydration due to increased fluid loss.

5. Pomegranate juice

They act as anti-inflammatory agents and help in soothing the stomach wall.

6. Baked sweet potato with ghee

Cooked starch in potatoes helps form solid stools and hence helps cure infective diarrhea.

Home remedies for Diarrhoea – Mucous

7. Liberal use of mint, cumin & lemon

These remedies help break down sticky mucous and help improve digestion.

8.Curd

Curd soothes the gut by providing probiotics that help reduce mucous.

9. Moong dal Sprouts

It decreases the frequency of loose stools in the same time providing the adequate nutrition. Moong Dal Soup is an excellent way to restore the lost fluids and prevent further diarrhea.

10.Avoid wheat, processed foods, spices

These foods may aggravate mucous production in the gut.

Please note – It is very important to maintain hydration during diarrheal episodes, so keep giving sips of water or juice at regular intervals.
