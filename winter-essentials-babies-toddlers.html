10 Winter Essentials for Babies and Toddlers

December 7, 2017 Leave a Comment

We’ve often heard of the term ‘winter wonderland’, and it often makes us think of snow covered peaks and fir trees. However, in India, winter can mean anything from snow fall to a blast of chilly air! Depending upon where you live in India, you may experience any kind of cool weather, with coastal areas generally having warmer climes than other places.

Regardless, there are still some common winter problems that affect us wherever we are, especially when it comes to little children. The lack of sunlight, lowered temperature, dry air and cold winds all contribute to illnesses or at the very least, some form of discomfort.

When it comes to babies, the challenges are more, since they are more vulnerable to seasonal changes and to top it off, they can’t articulate their concerns! So it makes sense for parents of young children to stay prepared with basic winter essentials for babies and toddlers. It also avoids the need to run out in the cold every time you need something!

10 Winter Essentials for Babies and Toddlers

1. Diapers

As it gets colder, babies tend to pee more, which means your usage of diapers goes up. If you’re using cloth diapers, the cold might make it difficult to dry them properly, which means it’s best to have a good stock ready to last you through laundry cycles.

2. Cap and Socks

Babies need to be protected from top to toe, which explains the need for a cap and socks! Babies who aren’t walking don’t really need shoes, just socks that are thick enough for the weather in your place. Similarly, go for a soft cap that isn’t excessively thick. Babies’ heads are often warmer than expected and a thick hat can make him uncomfortable.

3. Layered Clothes

In India, layered clothes are the ideal option since you can adjust them to the changing weather conditions. A simple shirt can be covered with a sweater and if it gets really cold, a shawl can be added. It saves you from all the trouble of dressing and undressing your baby repeatedly.

4. Sweaters

Along with your baby’s regular clothes, a sweater is a must have, especially if you plan to take her outdoors when its cold. Go for sweaters made from soft wool, that aren’t treated with chemicals or toxic dyes. Get one that’s large enough to go over baby’s existing clothes.

5. Blankets

Even if you live in a place where you don’t need blankets, chances are that the December night chills will require one for your baby. Soft and light quilts are good options and can be used throughout the year. Again, opt for ones made with 100% natural fabrics and don’t have padding falling out in places.

6. Moisturizer

Dry skin is one of the most common problems faced by babies during winter, with some being more susceptible than others. A good quality moisturizer is essential, so pick one that offers adequate protection against the rough, dry air. Make sure it’s a brand that is free from chemicals, fragrances and other substances that may cause allergies.

7. Laundry Detergent

Many of us don’t consider a laundry detergent as a winter essential, but it makes life much easier. Babies are messy, so you’ll find yourself having to clean their sweaters more than adults’. Having a custom laundry detergent will ensure baby sweaters remain clean and soft even after multiple washes.

8. Humidifier

During winters, the air can get very dry, and humidity can drop to low levels causing all kinds of health problems, like dry throat, breathing difficulty and skin problems. A humidifier helps to raise the humidity levels of a room, thereby offering comfort and relief from these issues.

9. Medicines

Be sure to stock up on all the medicines your baby may need, particularly pertaining to cough, cold and allergies. Talk to your pediatrician beforehand so that you can be well prepared in case of any health issue faced by your baby. Keep a medicine box exclusively for baby’s medicines so that they don’t get mixed up with the rest of the family’s.

10. Winter Foods

You’ve considered medicines for winter, but the most important and effective medicine is good nutrition! Winters call for heavier and heartier meals for your baby, so that they don’t get hungry too often. Winter foods should also help boost immunity and induce warmth. Banana Cereals, Multigrain Drinks and Masala Milk are great options for the cold weather.

When people think about preparing for winter, their thoughts are often restricted to sweaters and blankets, but now you know better! These winter essentials for babies and toddlers should keep your young ones warm, happy and protected for the entire season!
