10 Easy Christmas Crafts for Toddlers

December 24, 2015 Leave a Comment

“Christmas isn’t a season. It’s a feeling.” – Edna Ferber

So true, isn’t it? Christmas is an entire experience in itself, and encompasses a great range of positive emotions, like generosity, love and compassion. Focusing only on the commercial aspect of the festival deviates from the true spirit of Christmas, which is also a time meant for family bonding. If you have little toddlers at home, a great way to bond with them during the holidays is by doing crafts! So here we have 10 last minute Christmas crafts for toddlers, that are easy to make with basic craft supplies. So get crafting!

10 Easy Christmas Crafts for Toddlers

1. Santa Handprint Craft

Toddlers love sticking stuff, which is why collages are so popular with this age group! Try making this Santa handprint craft from Someday I’ll Learn, that uses multiple textures in a simple Santa collage.

2. Wrapping Paper Stocking

By now you’ve already probably finished wrapping your gifts, and have bits of scrap paper left over. Put them to good use with this fun and easy scrap paper stocking craft from No Time for Flash Cards.

3. Paper Plate Snowman

This craft from Crafty Morning is a great one for after the Christmas party, when you have a few paper plates left over. With some colored paper and googly eyes, you can make a really cute paper plate snowman that you can hang in your child’s room.

4. Popsicle Stick Christmas Tree

This is a fun craft from Happy Hooligans that uses a few different techniques like sticking, painting and tying. When your tree is ready, hang it on a prominent spot on your tree – your toddler will be so proud to see his creation hanging centrestage!

5. Pom Pom Painting Christmas Tree

Toddlers may not have the fine motor skills to handle a brush and paint fine strokes. It may be easier for them to use broader ‘brushes’ like pom poms to paint, like in this pom pom Christmas tree craft from Fantastic Fun and Learning.

6. Beaded Ornaments

This is an excellent craft for toddlers to exercise their motor skills, with impressive results! All you need are pipe cleaners and beads; show your toddlers how to thread them and watch them get addicted!

7. Pom Pom Hot Cocoa Mug

Winters are incomplete without hot cocoa, topped with marshmallows! As you get Christmas dinner ready, let the kids stay busy by sticking on pieces of colored paper and pom poms to make these cute little cocoa mugs from Crafty Morning. This is also a good activity to occupy the kids at your Christmas party.

8. Paper Plate Gingerbread Man Craft

This is one gingerbread man who won’t get up and run away! This craft from Happy Hooligans is simple, but may require a little more time, with a little more mess! With some paint and some new techniques, toddlers will be delighted at their creation!

9. Pine Cone Trees

These cute little Christmas trees from The Simple Parent are easy to make, but will still test your toddler’s skills! Hand the kids some pine cones, pipe cleaners and pom pom balls and let them let their creativity loose!

10. Thumbprint Candy Cane Craft

This is one of the simplest crafts your toddler can make, and also makes a great keepsake gift for grandparents! At Leapfrog & Ladybugs, you’ll find a step by step tutorial for making this colorful thumbprint candy cane craft.

Aren’t these really easy to make? And the best part is that you only need basic supplies, so no last minute rushing to the craft store! Just spread a lot of newspaper on the floor, gather everything you need and get crafting!
